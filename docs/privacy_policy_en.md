# Scorebook Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 19, 2026

Scorebook is an offline score tracking app and does not create accounts. Profile, friend, photo, game, note, and score data is stored only on the user's device and is not transmitted to the developer.

The free version may display limited advertising supplied by Google AdMob. Depending on the user's region and consent choices, Google may process advertising identifiers, IP address, device and app information, approximate location derived from IP, ad impressions and interactions, and consent signals. The app uses Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) to determine and collect applicable advertising consent before requesting ads. A one-time ad-free purchase stops future ad requests.

The optional `Remove Ads Forever` purchase is processed by Apple App Store or Google Play. AlpWare Studio does not receive payment-card details. The app processes the product identifier, transaction and receipt data, purchase status, and store-account entitlement information required to complete or restore the purchase. A local entitlement cache keeps ads disabled while offline. Store providers process purchases under their own terms and privacy policies.

Analytics and crash reporting are optional and disabled by default. If the user enables **Analytics and crash reports** in Settings, Google Firebase processes limited technical data on behalf of AlpWare Studio:

- Firebase Analytics receives app interactions, screen names, app/device information, and coarse technical metadata.
- Firebase Crashlytics receives crash stack traces, relevant application state, device and operating-system information, and installation identifiers needed to group and diagnose crashes.

Scorebook does not attach profile details, player or friend names, notes, photos, or scores to analytics events or crash reports. Firebase Analytics advertising-identifier collection and ad-personalization signals are disabled; advertising consent and identifiers used by AdMob are governed separately through UMP and the user's choices. The user can stop optional diagnostics at any time from Settings. Google may process this data on global infrastructure under its applicable terms and privacy documentation.

Photo library access occurs only when the user chooses a profile or friend photo. Backup and sharing operations are initiated by the user and transfer data to a destination selected through the operating system share sheet.

Users can permanently remove local game and profile data from Settings > Data and privacy > Delete all data. Uninstalling the app also removes its local app data from the device. Store-managed purchase history and entitlements are controlled through Apple App Store or Google Play and are not deleted by this action. Data previously transmitted to Firebase is retained and deleted according to Google's applicable retention policies.

For privacy questions, use the developer support contact shown on the store listing.
